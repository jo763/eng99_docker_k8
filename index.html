University Projects

This page details some of the key projects I have conducted at the University of Surrey.

Literature Review: Literature Review of Gastric Emptying Methodology and Mathematical Modelling using the 13C-Octanoic Acid Breath Test

As the title says, this was a literature review covering the topic of gastric emptying. This is a cross-departmental project with the department of nutrition and the literature review is currently being modified to prepare for publication. The review can be accessed here.

Design Project: Oligomerisation and separation plant

For this project, I designed the merox process, focusing on removing the mercaptans (sulphur containing compounds). This included research, design, equipment sizing, materials of construction selection and thermodynamic validation of the simulation packages. I was the safety, health and environment engineer for the overall design, completing dozens of DOW fire and explosive indices, a COMAH analysis and leading a HAZOP on a line. The report can be accessed here.

Advanced Equipment Design: Furnace Reboiler

My partner and I selected the project to complete the advanced design of a furnace reboiler. We produced a detailed process and mechanical design for a twin-helical thermosyphon fired-heater reboiler in a four-week period. This was one of the most technically challenging projects allocated that year. The report can be accessed here.

Webscraper - Professional Institute Events

This project web scrapes all the training events from various engineering institutes: Institute of Chemical Engineers, Institute of Mechanical Engineers, Institute of Civil Engineers etc. and formats the results into an excel file. This project uses the scrapy module, which whilst more complicated than typical approaches, allows concurrent requests, and hence a much faster crawl.

Linear graph reader

This is a quicker and more accurate method to measure datapoints from a graph. The user can upload an image of a graph, calibrate the axes and then they can select any point and will receive the correct reading. They can then optionally store the selected value in an excel file. The program also has the option of multiple screen-sizes. The source code can be found here.

Questions and Forfeits

The app is meant to see how well people know each other by asking questions about one of the players, whilst the other players need to guess the answer. After all the players have guessed, the player who was asked the question reveals the answer and those who got it incorrect forfeits (i.e. takes a drink or does a burpee). If there are more than 3 players, additional questions are added including challenge modes, to see who can beat whom in certain competitions. Improvements for the app, include the inclusion of more questions, as well as topics that can be selected. The game can be accessed here.

Shots and Ladders

The recalibration of the counter placement will be updated soon. Other improvement ideas include: more maps, different play modes etc. The game can be accessed here.
